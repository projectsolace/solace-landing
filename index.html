Our Motivation Behind Solace

Studies have shown that writing or speaking what’s on our minds is extremely beneficial to our sense of self and well-being. Unfortunately many people don't take the time out to write out what's on their minds and others are often embarrassed or unwilling to tell their problems to another person. This is the problem we are trying to solve.

With Solace we created an easier and more accurate method of self-introspection. Watson's response is sent back as percentiles so it compares users to the millions of responses it has received, allowing for the most accurate analysis. We take this analysis and create visual representations, allowing users to learn and discover more about themselves.

Mobile

Solace is an iOS application built using React Native. It harnesses the power of IBM Watson’s supercomputer to help users track their mental and emotional processes over time.

The way it works is simple. Open the app, log in or sign up...

...enjoy the beautiful views and inspirational message, hit the big red microphone button, and express yourself.

Users are encouraged to speak for at least one minute for Watson to generate an accurate analysis.

Watson sends back over 50 personality and emotional insights based on the recording.

To view their data, users can go to Charts and choose to filter data by week, month, or all time. Users can also see their total data to track how their recordings have changed over time.

Web

We wanted to add a functionality that allows administrators of the app to analyze data based on demographics.

This is why we also built a web portal for admins to understand general trends among different demographics. The web portal aggregates all data and groups them into separate demographic categories for admins to analyze.

Try It Out

Chrome Extension

A bonus feature we also added is a Chrome extension that makes it easy for you to interact with our app when it is inconvenient to talk on your phone.

When a thought suddenly pops up while you are browsing on your computer, you can immediately open up the Chrome extension and type away. After you finish, you can look up your analysis on your phone later.
